What You'll Experience

Participants live within the ecosystem they are studying, making the farm itself the classroom. Every day blends theory with real hands-on work.

🌿

Garden & Compost Systems

Raised bed design, companion planting, succession planting, and building compost systems including hot, cold, sheet mulch, and vermicomposting.

🍎

Orchard, Fruits & Berries

Food forest design, fruit tree pruning and feeding, berry ecology with raspberries, blueberries, and strawberries, plus pollinator companion species.

🍄

Mushroom Cultivation

Log inoculation, straw bed growing, indoor substrate bags. Learn lion's mane, oyster, and shiitake cultivation from spore to harvest.

🐝

Beekeeping & Pollinators

Hive ecology, queen health, seasonal cycles, and apiary visits with protective gear. Understand how pollination drives the entire farm ecosystem.

🌱

Biodiversity Walks

Guided walks through natural wildlife corridors, forest floor observation, and soil biology studies. See decomposition, fungi, and natural systems at work.

🍴

Farm-to-Table Living

Morning farm chores, sunrise garden walks, meals from the garden, evening bonfires, and optional family participation with kids biodiversity activities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need prior farming or gardening experience?

No experience is required. The 3-day and 7-day retreats are designed for beginners through experienced growers, with step-by-step instruction and hands-on guidance.

What accommodation options are available?

Guests may choose from a private farmhouse room, rustic bunkie, bell tent glamping, or BYOT camping depending on their preferred experience and budget.

Is the certificate included?

Yes. All participants who complete the course modules and hands-on activities receive a Straight Fin Farms Certificate in Practical Permaculture Design & Biodiversity Stewardship.

Are meals included?

Farm-to-table meals and refreshments can be included depending on the selected package and retreat format.

Cancellation Policy
